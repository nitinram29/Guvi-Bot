This recognition not only solidifies the standing of UI/UX as a high-paying career but also ensures sustained growth opportunities for professionals in the field.

Factors Influencing UI/UX Designer Salaries in India

It is important to understand the factors that influence your UI/UX salary before understanding the salary figures for different job roles. The key factors that determine the salaries of UI/UX professionals are:

1. Experience

Experience plays a crucial role in determining the salary of a UI/UX designer. Entry-level designers may start with a basic salary, but as they gain experience and expertise, their earning potential increases. Mid-level and senior designers with several years of experience can also command higher salaries, given they keep up with evolving trends and UI/UX advancements.

2. Skills and Expertise

The specific skills and expertise possessed by a UI/UX designer can also impact their salary. Designers with a strong command of design tools, prototyping, wireframing, and information architecture are in high demand and can negotiate higher salaries. Usually, Professionals with expertise in skills specific to the organization are paid more than professionals with basic skill sets.

3. Location

The location of a UI/UX designer can also affect their salary based on the distance, availability of resources & more. In India, Metro cities like Chennai, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad often offer higher UI/UX salaries compared to smaller cities and towns. This is due to the higher cost of living and increased demand for skilled designers in these urban hubs.

4. Reputation and Size of the Company

Another unnoticed reason for the increase/decrease in salary is the size and market share of the company, candidates apply for. Not just for UI/UX designers but for all the job roles, the respective company’s position in the market, its effective operations, and more such factors significantly impact the salary of the employees.

UI Roles and Their Rewarding Salaries

In a nutshell, UI designers are responsible for designing the visual elements of a product, ensuring a visually appealing and engaging user interface. In this section, let us explore some popular UI job roles and their salaries based on various factors like location, companies, and Experience.

1. UI Designer

The UI designer creates the visual elements of the user interface, including layout, color schemes, typography, and interactive elements. He develops visually engaging and consistent designs that align with the overall user experience and brand identity.

Salaries based on Locations:

UI designers with 2+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Lollipop UI/UX studio, Tech Mahindra, Zoho, Google, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

User interface design (Figma, Adobe XD), HTML, Adobe Photoshop, Visual Design software.

2. UI Design Intern

A UI Intern learns and practices to create wireframes and illustrate user interface designs. They reflect upon user feedback and expose themselves to case studies. Interns collaborate with product managers, developers, and other stakeholders to get inputs on how to improve the UI design.

Stipends based on Locations:

UI interns with appropriate skills and open to upskilling earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Lollipop UI/UX studio, Tech Mahindra, Zoho, Google, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

User interface design (Figma, Adobe XD), HTML, Adobe Photoshop, Visual Design software.

3. UI Developer/Front-End Developer

A UI Developer/Front-End Developer translates UI designs into code, building functional digital products.

Salaries Based on Location:

UI developers with 2+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Cognizant, Tech Mahindra, Accenture, Capgemini, IBM, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

UI Development, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Jquery.

4. Visual Designer

A Visual Designer focuses specifically on the visual aspects of the UI, emphasizing aesthetics, branding, and overall design cohesion.

Salaries Based on Location:

Visual Designers with 2+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in companies like Accenture, TCS, RR Donnelley, Amazon, Deloitte, Google, UX Reactor India, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, UI & UX design software, HTML.

5. UI Architect

UI Architect defines the architecture of the UI, ensuring that it meets both current and future requirements for a seamless user experience.

Salaries Based on Location:

UI Architects with 5+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Cognizant, Hexaware Tech, Wipro, Fiserv, IBM, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

UI Development, UX skills, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more.

UX Roles and Their Rewarding Salaries

UX Designers are responsible for the look and feel of a product, website, or app. They create user-friendly interactions and prototypes that represent how the product will look and function. In this section, let us explore some popular UX job roles and their salaries based on various factors like location, companies, and experience.

1. UX Designer

The UX designer conducts user research, creates personas, designs wireframes and prototypes, and collaborates with cross-functional teams like the UI design team to ensure a user-centric design.

Salaries based on Locations:

UX designers with 1+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Lollipop UI/UX studio, Cognizant, Accenture, Infosys, Google, Amazon, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

Adobe Photoshop, Visual Design, Adobe Illustrator, UX tools (Figma, Miro, Adobe XD), and more.

2. UX Intern

A UX intern gains hands-on experience, contributing to the various UX design processes within a company. He/she often works closely with UX designers, UI designers, Analysts, UX writers, and UX researchers. This helps the intern set their pace in UX design.

Stipend insights based on Locations:

UX Interns with appropriate skills and open to upskilling can earn rewarding salaries in companies like TCS, Amazon, Adobe, IBM, SAP, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

Adobe Photoshop, Visual Design, Adobe Illustrator, UX tools (Figma, Miro, Adobe XD), and more.

3. Usability Analyst

A Usability Analyst prioritizes evaluating the usability and user experience of the UI through testing and analysis. They conduct usability testing, gather user feedback, and provide insights to improve the design’s overall usability and effectiveness.

Salaries based on Locations:

Usability Analysts with 4+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in top companies like TCS, FIS, HCL, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

Analytical skills, communication, Adobe Photoshop, Visual Design, Adobe Illustrator, UX tools (Figma, Miro, Adobe XD), and more.

4. Information Architect

The Information architect generates sitemaps, user flows, and taxonomies to improve content organization and navigation.

Salaries based on Locations:

Information Architects with 5+ years of experience earn rewarding salaries in top companies like IBM, Cognizant, JP Morgan Chase, HP, Wipro, HCL Tech, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

Information Architecture, Reporting and communication skills, UX knowledge & skills, Data Management, and more.

5. UX Researcher

UX Researcher is responsible for conducting surveys, interviews, and observational studies to inform design decisions and ensure they align with user needs.

Salaries based on Locations:

UX Researcher with 2+ years of experience earns rewarding salaries in top companies like Nykaa, Adobe, Accenture, IBM, Google, IIFL Finance, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

User Empathy, Usability Testing, Quantitative and qualitative research, product design, and more.

6. UX Writer

A UX writer is a professional who crafts concise and effective content within digital interfaces, ensuring a seamless and user-friendly experience by maintaining the brand’s tone and voice.

Salaries based on Locations:

UX Writer with 2+ years of experience earns rewarding salaries in top companies like Nykaa, Adobe, Accenture, IBM, Google, IIFL Finance, and more.

Skills preferred by high-paying employers for this role:

User Empathy, Usability Testing, Content Strategy, User research, product design, and more.

Scope of UI/UX Jobs

The scope of UI/UX jobs is not confined to a singular industry or medium. As technology continues to advance, so does the demand for skilled designers capable of creating exceptional user experiences. Let’s find out which industries are popularly hiring UI/UX professionals.

E-Commerce and Retail

In the field of e-commerce, UI/UX design plays a pivotal role in creating user-friendly interfaces that drive conversions. From seamless navigation to persuasive product pages, UI/UX designers contribute to the overall success of online retail platforms.

Average Annual Salary : 8.5 LPA

Also Read: Top 8 Product-based Companies for UI/UX Designers [Freshers]

Mobile App Development

With the upsurge of smartphones, the demand for mobile app designers has increased. UI/UX designers in the mobile app development space are tasked with creating interfaces that optimize the limited screen real estate while delivering a delightful user experience.

Average Annual Salary : 9.0 LPA

Health-Tech and Wellness

In the health tech and wellness industry, UI/UX designers contribute to the development of user-friendly healthcare apps and platforms. The sensitivity of these applications requires a nuanced approach to design.

Average Annual Salary : 10.4 LPA

FinTech and Finance

In the field of FinTech, UI/UX designers play a crucial role in simplifying complex financial interactions. From banking apps to investment platforms, designers contribute to creating interfaces that instill trust and facilitate seamless transactions.

Average Annual Salary : 8.0 LPA

All salary numbers are sourced from websites like Ambitionbox and Glassdoor.

FAQs

1. Is UI/UX design a good career in India?

Yes. With the rising competition, Businesses are aimed at delivering exceptional user experiences. Top companies hire highly skilled UI/UX professionals to improve their digital accessibility for their businesses.

2. What is the salary range for entry-level UI/UX designers in India?

In India, Freshers with 0-1 year of experience can earn ₹3 Lakhs to ₹5 Lakhs per year on average. However, it might slightly differ based on factors like location, industry, reputation of the company, and more.

3. What is the scope of UX research Specialization?

With a specialization in UX research,  you can set up your ladder with rewarding opportunities and job positions like Research coordinator, Junior user researcher, Mid-level user researcher, Senior user researcher, Lead user researcher, and Head of user research.  

4. What are the skills required to become a UX Designer?

UX research, user empathy, interface design, user interviewing skills, visual design, and UX writing are the crucial skills required to become a UX designer.

5. Who can learn UI/UX?

Anyone with basic computer knowledge and creative thinking can learn UI/UX design. To get started, consider pursuing certifications or reputable courses in UI/UX design.
