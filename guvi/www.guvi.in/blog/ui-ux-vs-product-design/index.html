UI UX vs Product Design: 8 Insightful Healthy Comparisons

Jun 29, 2024 5 Min Read 718 Views

(Last Updated)

In the world of design, two disciplines play a crucial role in creating user-friendly and visually appealing products: UI UX design and product design.

While these two fields may seem similar at first glance, they have distinct differences in terms of their focus, methodologies, and skill sets. That is why, the debate of UI UX vs Product Design is an everlasting one.

In this article, we will delve into the key differences between UI UX vs product design, shedding light on their unique characteristics and contributions to the design process.

Getting into it…

UI UX design and product design are both integral parts of the design process, but they have distinct areas of focus and responsibilities.

UI UX designers primarily concentrate on creating seamless user experiences, while product designers are concerned with developing tangible products that meet consumer needs.

Understanding the differences between these two disciplines is essential to understanding the comparisons of UI UX vs product design, which enables us to optimize these design strategies and create successful products.

What is UI UX Design?

In the battle between UI UX vs product design, let us first understand about UI UX design. UI UX design, short for user interface and user experience design, revolves around creating digital products that provide meaningful and intuitive user experiences.

UI UX designers aim to enhance the usability, accessibility, and overall satisfaction of users when interacting with websites, applications, or other digital interfaces.

The Role of UI UX Designers

The first major thing that we need to understand in the UI UX vs product design debate is the role of the individuals. UI UX designers play a vital role in the design process by ensuring that products are user-friendly and visually appealing.

They are responsible for understanding user needs, conducting user research, creating wireframes, designing user interfaces, and conducting usability testing.

UI UX designers collaborate closely with other members of the design and development teams to create cohesive and engaging user experiences.

The Elements of UI UX Design

Next up in the comparison between UI UX vs product design, we have the elements. UI UX design encompasses several key elements that contribute to the overall user experience. These elements include the surface, skeleton, structure, scope, and strategy of the product.

The surface refers to the visual elements of the interface, such as images, buttons, and text. The skeleton represents the underlying structure of the product’s interfaces, while the structure defines how the features and functions of the product fit together.

What is Product Design?

Now that we have seen the former segment of UI UX vs product design, it is time to see the latter.

Product design involves creating physical or digital products that solve consumer problems and fulfill user needs. Product designers consider various factors, such as functionality, aesthetics, and cost, to develop products that are both appealing and practical.

The Role of Product Designers

Coming to the main part of the UI UX vs product design debate, Product designers are responsible for the entire design process, from conceptualization to production.

They collaborate with cross-functional teams to define product requirements, create prototypes, conduct market research, and ensure that the final product meets user expectations.

The Elements of Product Design

As we saw about the elements of UI UX in the previous section, now it is time to see the elements of Product design to gain a basic understanding of UI UX vs product design. This incorporates three essential elements: function, style, and cost.

  • Function refers to how users will interact with the product and the features that will help them achieve their goals.
  • Style focuses on creating aesthetically pleasing designs, incorporating elements such as color schemes and graphics.
  • Cost considers the overall production cost of the product and its competitiveness in the market.

UI UX vs Product Design: All The Important Comparisons

1. Job Requirements and Responsibilities

First and foremost in the comparison between UI UX vs product design is the jobs that they do. Let us see more about it in depth:

UI/UX:

  • UI UX designers and product designers have distinct job requirements and responsibilities that reflect the nature of their respective disciplines.
  • UI UX designers often need a strong understanding of user experience design principles, proficiency in design and prototyping tools, and experience in user research and competitor analysis.
  • They must be skilled in creating user stories, personas, and storyboards, interpreting data and feedback, and understanding interaction design principles.

Read: UI/UX Designer Job Description: Know-It-All

Product Design:

  • Product designers typically require a relevant undergraduate degree or formal qualification, along with professional experience in UX/UI design.
  • They should possess proficiency in design and prototyping tools, as well as expertise in design thinking and contributing to a design system.
  • Strong wireframing/prototyping skills, collaboration and communication abilities, and experience working on complex applications are also essential.

2. Focus of Work

Second, in our UI UX vs product design comparison, we have the focus of work:

UI/UX:

  • The focus of work differs between UI UX vs product design, reflecting their distinct priorities and objectives.
  • UI UX designers primarily concentrate on creating user-friendly and intuitive interfaces, ensuring that users can navigate products easily and efficiently.
  • They aim to optimize the user experience and enhance usability, focusing on factors such as information architecture, interaction design, and visual design.

Product Design:

  • Product designers, on the other hand, have a broader focus that encompasses both the functionality and aesthetics of the product.
  • They work on creating tangible solutions to consumer problems, incorporating elements of engineering, manufacturing, and user-centered design.
  • Product designers must consider the overall user experience while also paying attention to factors such as cost, marketability, and brand alignment.

3. Working Methodology

How individuals work in the two disciplines is an important factor to consider when we are comparing UI UX vs product design.

UI/UX:

  • UI UX designers and product designers employ different methodologies and approaches in their work.
  • UI UX designers follow a user-centered design process, which involves conducting user research, creating user personas, and developing wireframes and prototypes.
  • They iteratively test and refine their designs based on user feedback, ensuring that the final product meets user needs and expectations.

Product Design:

  • Product designers adopt a holistic approach, considering the entire product lifecycle from ideation to production.
  • They collaborate with cross-functional teams, conduct market research, and create prototypes to validate their design concepts.
  • Product designers also work closely with engineers, manufacturers, and marketing teams to ensure the successful launch and integration of the product.

4. Skill Set Needed

In UI UX vs product design comparison, we are going to see about the skills needed:

UI/UX:

  • UI UX designers and product designers require different skill sets to excel in their respective roles.
  • UI UX designers need strong design skills, including proficiency in industry-standard design and prototyping tools.
  • They must have a deep understanding of the UX design process, including user research, information architecture, interaction design, and visual design principles.

Product Design:

  • Product designers need a diverse range of skills, including UX/UI design, technical knowledge in engineering or manufacturing, and proficiency in design and prototyping tools.
  • They must be adept at applying design thinking methodologies, contributing to a design system, and creating functional designs across various devices.

5. Tools Used

Distinct tools are required for both the disciplines. Let us see some common tools in the UI UX vs product design comparison.

UI/UX:

  • Both UI UX designers and product designers utilize various tools and software to support their design processes.
  • UI UX designers commonly use graphic design software, prototyping tools (such as Figma), and web applications.
  • These tools enable them to create visually appealing designs, interactive prototypes, and testable user interfaces.

Product Design:

  • Product designers rely on 3D modeling software, computer-aided design (CAD) tools, and industrial design software to bring their product concepts to life.
  • CAD software allows them to create detailed digital models of products, ready for manufacturing and production.

6. Qualifications and Training

In the battle between UI UX vs Product design, it is crucial to understand the qualification and training each of them requires:

UI/UX:

  • UI UX designers and product designers often pursue different educational paths and training to acquire the necessary skills for their roles.
  • UI UX designers typically complete digital web design programs or attend UX design-related courses to gain a comprehensive understanding of user experience design.

Product Design:

  • Product designers may have backgrounds in industrial design, mechanical engineering, or software development.

7. Expected Salary

Last but not least in the comparison between UI UX vs product design, we have the most important factor, salary:

UI/UX:

  • The salary expectations for UI UX vs product design can vary based on factors such as experience, location, and industry.
  • UI UX designers often earn an average salary of around 7 LPA. However, salaries can range from 3 LPA to 14 LPA, depending on factors such as experience level, location, and the company’s size and industry.

Product Design:

  • Product designers, on the other hand, typically earn an average salary of approximately $80,000 per year.
  • However, salaries can range from 2.5 LPA to 25 LPA, depending on factors such as experience, industry, and the complexity of the products they work on.

While UI UX designers concentrate on creating user-friendly digital experiences, product designers develop tangible products that solve consumer problems. By understanding the key differences between UI UX vs product design, businesses can optimize their design strategies and create successful products that resonate with users.

In conclusion, UI UX vs product design is a healthy comparison which have distinct roles, methodologies, and skill sets, but both play vital roles in creating exceptional user experiences and innovative products. FAQs

What is the difference between UI UX and product design?

UI design focuses on the look and feel of a product, while UX design centers on the overall user journey and satisfaction. Product design encompasses both, integrating functionality and aesthetics.

What is the difference between UX and UI?

UX is the holistic experience users have with a product, including navigation and usability, whereas UI refers to the visual elements and design components. Both are crucial for a seamless user experience.

What is product design?

Product design involves creating and optimizing products, considering functionality, aesthetics, and user experience. It combines elements of UI and UX design to deliver a comprehensive solution.

Does UI UX require coding?

No, UI UX design does not necessarily require coding. While some designers may have coding skills, it’s not a mandatory aspect of UI UX work. Designers often collaborate with developers to bring their designs to life.
