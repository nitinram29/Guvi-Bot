FAQS

What is CAD & BIM?

CAD software allows designers and engineers to create precise and detailed 2D and 3D models of objects, buildings, or mechanical parts. It provides tools for drafting, modeling, visualization, simulation, and documentation, enabling users to efficiently design and communicate their ideas.

On the other hand, BIM, or Building Information Modeling is a 3D-based, intelligent process that creates a digital representation of a commercial infrastructure’s real physical and functional dimensions from a multitude of angels. 

Are CAD and BIM important for a Civil Engineering & Architectural career?

Yes. CAD and BIM play crucial roles in enhancing productivity, accuracy, and efficiency in civil engineering and architectural practices. They streamline the design process, improve collaboration among project stakeholders, minimize errors and clashes, facilitate project coordination, and enable better decision-making. Proficiency in CAD and BIM software is highly valued in the industry and can greatly benefit individuals pursuing careers in civil engineering and architecture.
